Understanding Eating Disorders

Eating disorders are serious mental health conditions that can affect anyone. Recovery is possible with the right support, treatment, and understanding.

What are Eating Disorders?

Eating disorders are mental health conditions characterized by abnormal eating habits that negatively affect a person's physical and mental health. They often involve preoccupation with food, weight, and body image.

Common Types

  • Anorexia Nervosa: Severe restriction of food intake
  • Bulimia Nervosa: Binge eating followed by purging
  • Binge Eating Disorder: Frequent overeating without purging
  • Other Specified Feeding or Eating Disorders (OSFED)

Common Symptoms

  • Extreme concern about weight and body shape
  • Restrictive eating or binge eating
  • Purging behaviors (vomiting, laxatives)
  • Excessive exercise
  • Body image distortion
  • Physical health complications

Treatment Options

Treatment typically involves a multidisciplinary approach including therapy, nutritional counseling, medical monitoring, and sometimes medication. Early intervention improves recovery outcomes.
